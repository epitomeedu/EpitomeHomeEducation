Epitome Explained

About Our Founder

A grateful mother of 2 wonderful individuals; a man, aged 24 years and a high spirited, curious, 6 year old boy. I am a lifetime student having earned degrees from several accredited NY State Institutions such as Syracuse University and State University of New York @ Stony Brook.

Forever a student first, I am an educator at heart, and have longed for decades to put into place a micro-school that treats each child as an individual with a localized purpose.

Since my big son was small I have pined to be the teacher that allows these children FREEDOM from a system that does not serve them or their families. As a longtime entrepreneur who believes deeply in the power of small, intentional communities, I listened to God as He spoke to me about West Texas.

After years as a chef, bakery owner and property manager, the list of interests never ends. I love to bake and cook obviously but I am an avid outdoors person, knitter, reader, seamstress, fashionista, woodworker, writer — the list will go on forever.

Being an entrepreneur, I learned firsthand the value of creativity, discipline, and especially failing forward. Life has also taught me resilience. As a military widow and an expat who has lived and worked across cultures, I’ve experienced how important stability, compassion, and strong support systems are — especially for children.

This micro-school was born from that vision. I wanted to create a place where children are known, supported, and encouraged to learn at their own pace — where education is personal, practical, and rooted in real life.

About Our Micro-School

A safe, enriching learning environment designed to feel like home, with the structure and intention of school.

Epitome offers small class sizes, home-cooked meals, hands-on enrichment, and meaningful relationships between students and staff.

From outdoor learning spaces to music, cooking, and skill-building, students are supported as whole people — not just learners.

Our Vision & Community

Community, connection, and collaboration are central to Epitome.

Families are partners here. We grow together, learn together, and support one another.

Our goal is to raise confident, capable children who feel safe, valued, and inspired.
